Ship-Water Interaction System

Deck wash, interior flooding, bailing, and storm survival — the Sea of Thieves ship-in-danger loop

Ship Water Interaction — Cross-Section View

Deck Wash Mechanic

When wave height at a ship's gunwale sample points exceeds the deck height, water flows onto the deck. The flow rate uses the same orifice formula as the existing FloodingSystem: Q = Cd · L · √(2g · overtopping_depth) where L is the gunwale length segment exposed. This water enters the topmost compartment of the ship's FloodingComponent.

During storms, larger waves + ship roll combine to dramatically increase overtopping. A ship broadside to storm waves takes much more deck wash than one heading into them — heading matters.

Interior Flooding — Ties to Existing FloodingSystem

The existing FloodingSystem already handles compartment water levels, cross-flow between connected compartments, and CoM shift causing listing. We extend it with two new ingress sources:

  • Deck wash — water over the gunwale enters topmost compartments
  • Open hatches — if hatches/doors are open and submerged, water flows in at full orifice rate. Closing hatches is a player action that seals compartments.

The existing free surface effect (partially-flooded compartments reduce stability) makes this especially dangerous — a little water sloshing on one deck can start a feedback loop of listing → more water on low side → more listing.

Bailing & Repair — The Player Loop

Action Type Rate Notes
Bilge pump Automatic (passive) Slow — 0.05 m³/s Handles light spray; overwhelmed by breaches or storm wash
Bail bucket Manual (interact) Fast — 0.2 m³/scoop, ~1 scoop/sec Player scoops water and throws overboard. Requires being in the flooded compartment. Occupies both hands.
Repair kit Manual (interact) 3–5 sec channel per plank Seals hull breach — stops ingress at source. Temporary patch; permanent repair at dock.
Close hatch Manual (interact) Instant Seals a door/hatch to prevent water flow between compartments or from deck

Sinking Progression

Ships don't just pop underwater — they progressively sink as flooding overwhelms buoyancy. The existing BuoyancySystem handles this naturally: as compartments flood, total mass increases, the ship sits lower in the water, which exposes more breaches/hatches to water, which floods faster. The player's job is to break this feedback loop by bailing and repairing.

  • Warning stage — bilge alarm event when any compartment >30% flooded
  • Danger stage — ship listing noticeably, deck awash, multiple compartments flooding
  • Sinking stage — deck goes below waterline; published ShipSinkingEvent. Players must abandon ship. Existing CapsizeEvent fires if roll exceeds 60°.

Interior Water (Player Experience)

Inside the ship's interior physics world, flooded compartments have a rising water plane. When water level reaches the player's feet, wading mechanics activate. If it reaches their chest, they start swimming — inside the ship. The same SwimmingSystem state machine applies in interior worlds, just with the compartment's water level as the "surface" instead of the ocean's wave height.
