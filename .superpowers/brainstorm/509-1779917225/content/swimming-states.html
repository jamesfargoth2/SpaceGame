Swimming System — State Machine

Player water states and transitions, Sea of Thieves style

Player Water State Machine

State Descriptions

DRY Normal FPS movement. PlayerMovementSystem has full control. No water forces applied.
WADING Feet to waist in water. Walk speed reduced proportional to depth. Drag force applied to lower body. Camera stays dry. Can still jump.
SURFACE Treading water — camera bobs with wave height. WASD moves horizontally, auto-buoyancy keeps head above water. Swim speed ~2 m/s. Stamina drains slowly. Space = stay at surface, Crouch = dive.
DIVING Actively swimming downward. Breath meter starts counting. Camera look direction drives movement direction (like flying). Sprint = swim faster (more stamina drain). Buoyancy pulls you up — you must actively swim down.
SUBMERGED Deep water (>5m). Same as DIVING but requires gear for extended stays. Depth zone effects kick in (visibility, pressure). Without gear, auto-ascent after breath runs out. With gear, O₂ tank determines duration.
DROWNING Breath/O₂ depleted. Health drains rapidly. Player floats upward automatically (unconscious). If they reach the surface, health drain stops and breath slowly refills. If health hits 0, death.

Sea of Thieves Feel — Key Behaviors

  • Wave bobbing at surface — camera Y tracks WaveSystem.getHeight() + small offset. Smooth interpolation, not snapped.
  • Auto-surface — when player stops providing dive input, buoyancy gradually pulls them up. Not instant — a gentle float.
  • Momentum in water — movement has inertia. Stop swimming and you drift. Current pushes you. Storms create stronger currents.
  • Camera tilt — subtle roll when swimming sideways on wave slopes. Camera smoothly tilts with the water surface normal.
  • Surface tension transition — brief camera blur/refraction effect when crossing the surface plane (entering/exiting water).
