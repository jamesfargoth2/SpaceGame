Asset Sourcing Strategy

Right now, ships are fully procedural (no external model files exist). What's your intended approach for characters, buildings, interior props, and foliage?

A

Hybrid — Procedural Ships + Imported Models for Everything Else

Keep ships procedurally generated (infinite variety, zero art debt). Import hand-crafted or purchased .glTF models for characters, NPC crew, interior props (chairs, consoles, lockers), buildings, and foliage. This is the most practical path — huge variation where it matters, fidelity where the player actually looks closely.

Pros

  • Ships stay infinite / seeded
  • Characters and props get real fidelity
  • Can use asset store purchases
  • Skeletal animation works out of the box

Cons

  • Two pipelines to maintain
  • Need to source/buy character models
  • glTF integration (gdx-gltf) not yet wired in
B

Fully Procedural — Generate Everything in Code

Extend the existing procedural approach to characters (parametric bodies), buildings (modular room packing), and props (primitive-built furniture). No external models at all — everything is seeded and generated at runtime.

Pros

  • Single unified pipeline
  • Truly infinite variation at all scales
  • No art asset dependency ever

Cons

  • Procedural characters are very hard to make look good
  • Huge implementation complexity
  • Animation is extremely difficult without skeletal rigs
  • Risk: looks primitive or uncanny
C

Full External Models — Import Everything Including Ships

Abandon procedural ship generation and move to a full hand-crafted or purchased model pipeline. Ships, characters, props, and buildings all come from .glTF files. Procedural variation achieved via modular swapping (hull sections, weapon hardpoints, livery) rather than geometry generation.

Pros

  • Highest visual quality possible
  • Easiest animation pipeline
  • Single unified asset system

Cons

  • Loses infinite procedural ship variety
  • Massive art production cost
  • Large rewrite of ship pipeline
